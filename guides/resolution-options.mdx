When creating your Star Wars crawl, you can choose between three resolution options: SD, HD, and 4K. Each offers different benefits depending on how you plan to use your video.

## Available resolutions

The Star Wars Crawl Creator offers three quality tiers to match your specific needs:

  </Card>
  <Card title="HD" icon="film">

## When to use each resolution

Choosing the right resolution depends on where and how you'll display your crawl.

### SD (Standard Definition)

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Best for">
    - Quick social media posts
    - Email attachments with size limits
    - Mobile-only viewing
    - Fast generation when time is critical
    - Testing and previewing your text before committing to higher quality
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Considerations">
    - Lower visual quality, especially on large screens
    - Text may appear less crisp
    - Not ideal for professional contexts
    - Smaller file size for easier sharing
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Info>
  SD is perfect when you need something quick or when file size matters more than maximum quality.
</Info>

### HD (High Definition)

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Best for">
    - Online presentations and meetings
    - YouTube and social media uploads
    - Website embedding
    - Most TV and computer screens
    - General-purpose use
    - Balancing quality and file size
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Considerations">
    - Excellent quality for most screens
    - Sharp, clear text that's easy to read
    - Reasonable file size
    - The "sweet spot" for most users
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Tip>
  HD is the recommended choice for most projects. It provides excellent quality without excessive file sizes.
</Tip>

### 4K (Ultra High Definition)

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Best for">
    - Large screen displays and projectors
    - Professional video productions
    - Film festivals or theatrical screenings
    - Future-proofing your content
    - Maximum text clarity and detail
    - Projects where quality is paramount
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Considerations">
    - Largest file size
    - May take longer to download
    - Requires 4K-capable playback devices to see full benefit
    - Overkill for small screens or web-only use
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Warning>
  4K files are significantly larger than HD. Make sure you have adequate storage and bandwidth for downloading and sharing.
</Warning>

## Quality and file size trade-offs

Understanding the differences helps you make the best choice:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Visual quality comparison">
    **Text clarity:**
    - **SD:** Readable but may show pixelation on screens larger than tablets
    - **HD:** Sharp and clear on most displays up to 55" screens
    - **4K:** Crystal clear even on large displays and projectors
    
    **Star field detail:**
    - **SD:** Background stars visible but less defined
    - **HD:** Clear starfield with good depth
    - **4K:** Maximum detail in space background and subtle effects
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="File size expectations">
    While exact sizes vary based on crawl length, here's what to expect:
    
    **Relative file sizes:**
    - **SD:** Smallest files, easiest to share via email
    - **HD:** 3-4x larger than SD, still manageable for most uses
    - **4K:** 8-10x larger than SD, may require file transfer services
    
    <Note>
      The video you receive will be delivered to your email. Very large 4K files may be delivered via a download link rather than direct attachment.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Playback compatibility">
    **SD:**
    - Plays on any device
    - Minimal bandwidth required for streaming
    - Fastest loading times
    
    **HD:**
    - Plays on all modern devices
    - Requires moderate bandwidth for smooth streaming
    - Standard for most web video
    
    **4K:**
    - Requires 4K-capable display to see full benefit
    - Needs strong bandwidth for streaming
    - May require transcoding for some older devices
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Choosing the right resolution

Use this decision tree to select the best option:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Consider your display">
    **Where will this play?**
    - Small screens (phones, tablets): SD is sufficient
    - Computer monitors, TVs: HD recommended
    - Large screens, projectors, theaters: Choose 4K
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Consider your purpose">
    **How will you use it?**
    - Social media, casual sharing: SD or HD
    - Professional presentations: HD
    - Production work, archival: 4K
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Consider file handling">
    **How will you share it?**
    - Email attachment: SD
    - Cloud storage, streaming: HD
    - Direct download, no size constraints: 4K if needed
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Can you change resolution later?

Each video generation creates a single resolution. If you need your crawl in multiple resolutions:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Submit the form again">
    Fill out the form with identical text but select a different resolution.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Use the same email">
    You can use the same email address to receive multiple versions.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Wait for delivery">
    Each resolution will be generated and delivered separately within a few minutes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  There's no way to "upgrade" an existing video file. Each resolution must be generated from scratch by resubmitting the form.
</Info>
